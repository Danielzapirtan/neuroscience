Mirror Neurons in Social Learning

Posted on October 7, 2025

Consider a neural circuit where mirror neurons fire both when performing an action and when observing the same action performed by others. This creates a neural basis for imitation learning without requiring conscious effort. The system maintains coherence because the same neuronal populations encode both execution and observation.

Posted on August 27, 2025

In this post we analyze gamma oscillations in cortical networks.

Let O be a 100-neuron cortical microcircuit.

Let us measure 20 overlapping subsets of 5 neurons each, recording their firing patterns in the temporal sequence they become active.

Then O exhibits gamma-band synchronization if and only if the phase coherence across the 20 subsets exceeds 0.95. (This works because e2πift≡1 (when f=40Hz) for binding to occur.)
